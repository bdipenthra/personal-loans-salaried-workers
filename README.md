# Examining the Effects of Personal Loans on Salaried Workers

**Academic Project | B.Com Corporate Secretaryship | Loyola College, Chennai | March 2025**

---

## Project Overview

This project investigates how personal loans affect the financial well-being, spending behaviour, and stress levels of salaried employees. Data was collected through a structured questionnaire survey of 53 respondents across Chennai and other cities, then cleaned, structured, and analysed in Microsoft Excel.

---

## Key Questions Explored

- Why do salaried workers take personal loans?
- Do personal loans improve or worsen financial stability?
- What is the relationship between multiple loans and debt traps?
- How aware are borrowers of interest rates and repayment terms?

## Key Findings

- **30.2%** of respondents had taken a personal loan in the past 5 years
- **Medical emergencies** (32%) and **education expenses** (28%) were the top reasons for borrowing
- **46.9%** reported no significant financial impact; **22.4%** experienced increased financial burden
- **48.1%** cited unexpected financial emergencies as the biggest repayment challenge
- **21.2%** had taken multiple loans simultaneously
- **65.3%** believed stricter lending regulations would help prevent debt traps
- A significant **31.4%** rated their understanding of loan terms at level 2 out of 5 — indicating low financial awareness among borrowers
